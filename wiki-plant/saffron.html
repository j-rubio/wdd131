Saffron

Additional Info

Common name: Saffron.

Scientific plant name: Crocus sativus.

Origin: It is mainly believed that saffron originated in Iran. Nevertheless, Greece and Mesopotamia have also been suggested as possible regions of origin.

Harvest season: It is an autumn-flowering perennial plant.

Production: Almost all saffron grows in a belt from Spain to India and Pakistan in the east. Today, 90% of the global production takes place in Iran, but due to international political sanctions, the list is led by Spain in 2021 with 396,417 Kg.
Source: World Bank Organization Spices; saffron exports by country | 2021 (worldbank.org)

Health benefits: Saffron constituents, such as safranal, crocetin, and crocin, are believed to affect mental depression and possibly cardiovascular risk factors. However, there is still no high-quality clinical evidence for such effects.

History of plant usage and discovery: Saffron-based pigments are found in 50,000-year-old depictions of prehistoric places in northwest Iran. The Sumerians later used wild-growing saffron in their remedies and magical potions. Over thousands of years, many cultures wove saffron threads into textiles, ritually offered to divinities. Also, it was used in dyes, perfumes, medicines, and body washes. Even Alexander the Great used Persian saffron in his infusions, rice, and baths to cure his battle wounds.
